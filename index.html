Who We Are

A Brotherhood Forged in Spirit

It began, as so many great institutions do, with a humble young man, a worthy son, and a singular conviction: that whiskey deserved more than to be consumed — it deserved to be celebrated. From the crossroads of Terrebonne Drive and County Road X in Wausau, Wisconsin, the Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin was willed into existence in the year of our Lord 2019.

What followed was the assembly of 24 gentleman connoisseurs united not merely by a love of the dram, but by the deeper truths whiskey unlocks — camaraderie, candor, laughter, and the occasional drunken haiku.

"In the water of life, there is truth."

— Motto of the Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin

Our society year runs from the eve of the NFL Draft to the eve of the next — a year measured not in calendars, but in drams savored and moments seized. The Society convenes in sport coat (preferably tweed), in fellowship, and in the pursuit of whiskey wisdom.

Whiskey procured for the gatherings of the Society shall only be savored during our illustrious meetings. Members are entitled to 1.5-ounce pours from each bottle present. The revered elixir may be consumed neat, on the rocks, or with water, as the drinker sees fit. "Whiskey" shall encompass any variety deemed appropriate by the board — including whiskey, whisky, bourbon, and Scotch.

All club directors are required to don a sport coat (preferably tweed) at any meeting. Newly initiated members must wear a button-down shirt and tie. First-time offenders will be mocked, ridiculed, and required to purchase a bottle of whiskey for the membership. Second-time offenders may be stripped of rank and possibly expelled at the Commodore's discretion.

Members must be at least 21 years of age, act responsibly during and after meetings, and abstain from whiskey snobbery. Members absent from a meeting forfeit their whiskey portion to those in attendance. Membership privileges last the duration of the Society year — from the first round of the NFL Draft to the eve of the next.

A Word From the Founder

The Commodore's Corner

The Commodore

Peter Gelhar

Commodore & Founding Member · Est. 2019 · Wausau, Wisconsin

Friends, gentlemen, and honored guests —

Welcome to the Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin.

I'll be honest with you — and honesty, much like a fine dram, is something we hold in high regard here. This whole thing started as an idea. One of many, if you ask anyone who knows me. I have ideas the way Wisconsin has winters: frequently, with varying degrees of severity, and occasionally leaving those around me wondering what exactly they've gotten themselves into.

This one, though. This one worked.

What began in 2019 at the crossroads of Terrebonne Drive and County Road X — with a bottle, a charter, and a handful of gentlemen willing to take a chance on something — has grown into a brotherhood I am genuinely proud of. Twenty-four members strong. A motto that means something. Events that people actually show up to. Even a website, apparently.

We are not a drinking club. We are a Society. There is a difference, and it matters to us. We gather to learn, to share, to argue respectfully about whether Islay or Speyside deserves the crown, and to remind ourselves that the best moments in life are the ones shared with good people over something worth savoring.

So whether you're a member in good standing, a guest of a member, or simply someone who stumbled upon this page through some mysterious alignment of fate and search algorithms — you are welcome here. Pour yourself something worthy. Stay awhile.

In Aqua Vitae Veritas,

Peter Gelhar
Commodore, Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin

Gregory Gelhar

Member · Elder Statesman · Official Lodging of the Commodore

Every great society has one — the older, wiser figure who joined reluctantly, surveyed the room with measured skepticism, and then never left. Gregory Gelhar is that man. Brother to the founders in spirit if not in caution, Greg arrived with reservations and departed with a jacket and a standing invitation.

A man of refined tastes, Greg’s passions are simple and non-negotiable: golf, a lawn so immaculately manicured it borders on the spiritual, and whiskey — always whiskey. He has been known to speak about his short game with the same reverence most men reserve for newborns and vintage Scotch.

Greg holds a distinction that few members can claim — he serves as the official lodging of the Commodore when Society business brings the founder to town. This act of hospitality alone has secured his legacy. The Society does not forget those who keep the Commodore comfortable and well-rested.

A word to the wise: Greg is unreachable on Sundays. Don’t ask why. Don’t test it. Simply respect it as you would a well-established house rule or a particularly peaty Islay malt — not for everyone, but beyond question.

One final note: Greg likes golf.

Neil Doherty

Member · Permanent Chapter President, New York Area

There are men who bring whiskey to the table. Then there are men who bring whiskey, a legal brief, and the unshakeable confidence of someone born within earshot of the Atlantic Ocean. Neil Doherty is the latter.

Hailing from the greatest city on the planet — New York City — Neil carries with him the particular refinement that only comes from a life lived between Manhattan boardrooms and the boardwalk at Coney Island. Posh and gritty in equal measure, he is the Pop Whiskey Society’s answer to a question nobody thought to ask, and the answer turns out to be spectacular.

A legal wizard of the first order, Neil serves as the Society’s de facto counsel on matters ranging from the procedural to the profound — and everything in between. When the Society has needed clarity, contract, or sheer intellectual firepower, Neil has delivered. Think of him as our Robert Kardashian, except the verdict is always in favor of a good dram.

As Permanent Chapter President of the New York Area Chapter, Neil has carried the Society’s banner into the five boroughs and beyond, evangelizing the gospel of fine whiskey to anyone willing to listen — and a few who weren’t.

Do not mistake his polish for softness. This is a man who loves Coney Island. There’s grit under those cufflinks.

Peter Gelhar

Commodore · Founding Member · The Man With The Ideas

Peter Gelhar has had a lot of ideas. A remarkable, some might say alarming, number of ideas. Business ventures, schemes, propositions, and grand plans have flowed from this man with the reliable frequency of a well-aged cask — and like most good whiskey, the results have varied. Some ideas have faded quietly into the night. Others have caused mild concern among friends and family. And then there is this one.

The Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin is, by any reasonable measure, Peter Gelhar's finest hour. Born in 2019 from equal parts genuine passion and characteristic audacity, the Society has grown into something that even its founder could not have fully anticipated — a brotherhood of 24 gentlemen bound by camaraderie, ceremony, and the shared conviction that whiskey deserves to be taken seriously, even when everything else does not.

As Commodore, Peter presides over all Society matters with the authority of a man who knows he built the thing and the humility of a man who knows it has long since outgrown him. He sets the agenda, pours the first dram, and reserves the right to declare any gathering officially adjourned. He is simultaneously the Society's greatest champion and its most enthusiastic participant — often the last one still standing at the end of the evening, glass in hand, already describing his next idea.

His whiskey of choice says everything you need to know: Balvenie Caribbean Cask 14 — a classic Speyside that spent its final years in rum casks, emerging with unexpected warmth, a touch of the exotic, and a sweetness that catches you slightly off guard.

It is, in short, the Peter Gelhar of whiskies.

James Baranowski

Loyalist · The Gatekeeper · Minister of Supply

There are members of the Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin who attend the meetings. There are members who plan the meetings. And then there is James Baranowski — the man who makes the meetings worth attending, the room worth entering, and the evening worth remembering.

Jim is a liquor and beer salesman by trade, which means he didn't just join a whiskey society — he came home. While the rest of the membership pores over tasting notes and debates finish lengths, Jim is three calls ahead, quietly ensuring that whatever the Society desires will be procured, delivered, and priced in a manner that makes the Commodore smile. He is, in the truest sense of the word, connected.

But here is the thing about Jim that no title or job description can fully capture: the man knows everyone. Not in the casual, vaguely familiar way most people mean when they say that. Jim knows everyone the way a mayor knows a city — by name, by preference, by the story behind the story. Walk into any room in Wausau with James Baranowski and you will leave three hours later having met twelve people you didn't know and somehow feeling like old friends with all of them. He has a gift for people that is frankly almost unfair.

It is this quality — more than the access to rare bottles, more than the industry connections, more than the generous pours — that makes Jim irreplaceable to the Society. A brotherhood is only as strong as the bonds between its members, and Jim has always been the man who deepens those bonds. He remembers the things people say when they think no one is listening. He shows up. He makes the call.

A loyalist from the Society's earliest days, Jim has been present for every chapter — the triumphs, the questionable decisions, and the occasional gathering that shall not be named in polite company.

And when the question inevitably arises — as it always does, late in the evening, when someone spots something extraordinary on a shelf or in a catalogue and dares to wonder aloud — Jim answers before it's even fully asked.

The deal is never done. If Jim can't get it, no one can.

Jeffrey Schuster

OG Member · Billiards Savant · First Man Tried

Every society needs a character who tests its boundaries, and the Pop Whiskey Society of Wisconsin found its in Jeffrey Schuster. An original member from the earliest days, Schuster has occupied a peculiar and uniquely precarious position within the brotherhood — perpetually on the edge, perpetually one absence away from formal proceedings, and perpetually, somehow, still here.

Schuster holds the dubious distinction of being the first member in Society history to be brought up on formal charges. The accusation: chronic and flagrant absenteeism, a pattern of ditching club functions so reliable it had become something of a running joke — until it wasn't. The trial was real. The charges were read aloud. And when the votes were cast, Jeffrey Schuster was saved — barely — by the very peers he had spent years avoiding.

It is a testament to the man's singular gift that he survived at all. Jeffrey Schuster is, by universal and uncontested agreement, the bullshitter of the group. His stories are long. His excuses are elaborate. His capacity for spin would make a career politician blush. And yet — and this is the part that confounds everyone — it works. Not always. Not on everyone. But just enough, just often enough, to keep him in the fold.

Where Schuster commands genuine and unironic respect, however, is at the billiards table. His skill in 3-ball is the stuff of quiet legend within the Society — a domain where the bullshit falls away and something real emerges. At the table, Jeffrey Schuster is focused, formidable, and surprisingly graceful. It is, perhaps, the one arena where the man's reputation is built entirely on merit.

An OG member hanging on by the edge — but hanging on all the same. The Society wouldn't be the same without him, though it will never admit this to his face.
